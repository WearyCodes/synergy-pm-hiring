About Synergy

A builder's builder, rooted in Chicago

Synergy Construction Group has spent 16+ years earning a reputation as one of Chicago's most trusted general contractors. We don't specialize in one thing — we're built to move across sectors, from transit-oriented apartment communities to neighborhood restaurants to senior living facilities.

The Role

What you'll own

Our team takes pride in ownership, integrity, and the quality of what we build. You'll work alongside ownership and senior construction leaders on the construction side of projects—gaining real accountability, building strong relationships, and making a meaningful impact on the built environment.

Day-to-Day Responsibilities

  • Manage all work in construction through final closeout and punch list.
  • Build and maintain project schedules, cost reports, and forecasts with full budget accountability
  • Lead weekly owner, architect, and subcontractor meetings — and keep them moving
  • Review and negotiate subcontractor bids, scopes of work, and change orders
  • Coordinate trade partner sequencing to protect the critical path
  • Manage the full documentation chain: RFIs, submittals, ASIs, meeting minutes
  • Navigate permitting, inspections, and municipal sign-offs
  • Identify schedule and cost risks early — and drive resolution before they compound

How You'll Work

  • Initially, with support and guidance, with increasing ownership and decision-making as you grow in the role
  • Across different types of active projects
  • Split between the office (Chicago, Lombard) and job sites
  • With a lean, high-trust team where your judgment is respected and your wins are visible
  • On projects that get press coverage and change neighborhoods — the kind of work worth doing

Who We're Looking For

Qualifications

We care more about your track record than your resume format. Here's what matters.

Required

  • 5+ years of commercial construction project management in the Chicago market
  • Demonstrated experience managing $1M–$20M+ budgets from start to closeout
  • Proficiency with Procore, MS Project, or comparable scheduling and PM tools
  • Ability to read and interpret full construction document sets
  • Strong subcontractor management and negotiation skills
  • Fluency navigating Chicago permitting and inspection processes
  • Valid driver's license and availability for regular site visits across Chicagoland

Nice to Have

  • Degree in Construction Management, Civil Engineering, or Architecture
  • Experience across multiple sectors — multi-family, healthcare, and retail all a plus
  • Background on ground-up transit-oriented or mixed-use development
  • PMP, LEED AP, or OSHA 30 certification
  • Experience managing union and open-shop labor on the same project
  • Relationships with Chicago-area subcontractors and inspectors
